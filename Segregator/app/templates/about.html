The project "IoT Based Waste Segregator" has been co-developed by the students of Dayananda Sagar College of Engineering , Bangalore namely , Boudhayan Dev, Aditya Agarwal, Chinmay Hebbal , Aishwarya H.S. The device detects , identifies and segregates waste items without any human intervention .
The prime motivation behind this project has been the problem that affects us all i.e Waste Management . The million tons of waste that are generated daily do not get properly segreagted and as a result, a large portion of the non biodegradable waste are mixed with biodegradable ones and are dumped in landmines . This ultimately piles up and their effect on the environment esp. Soil and Groundwater resources becomes devastating . Thus , we have come up with the idea that if we can segregate the waste items at the grassroots levels itself i.e households , then the percentage of mixup can be reduced by a great margin . This in turn will also help in increasing the amount of recyclable items from the waste heap.
We have used Raspberry Pi 2 B development board for this project . It consists of a network of servo motors for controlling its arm and the rotating flap , inside the compartment . It is also equipped with a moving base that is powered by DC motors . The image recognition utilises the Google Vision API . The working demo can be found in the "Home" section .
